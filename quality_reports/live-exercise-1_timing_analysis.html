Timing Analysis: live-exercise-1.qmd

File: exercises/live/live-exercise-1.qmd Exercise Type: Live (group work) Target Time: 15-20 minutes Review Date: 2026-02-09

Executive Summary

Estimated Completion Time: 25-33 minutes (including optional part d) Without Optional Parts: 20-28 minutes (parts a,b,c,e,f) Deviation from Target: +25% to +65% over 20-minute target

Status: ⚠️ OVER TIME (CRITICAL if including part d; MEDIUM without it)

Recommendation: Mark part (d) explicitly as “optional/challenge” OR remove parts (e) and (f) to fit 20-minute window.

Time Breakdown by Part

Part (a): Marginal consumer and demands

Tasks: 1. Write indifference condition 2. Solve for x* (quadratic algebra) 3. Derive q1(p1,p2) and q2(p1,p2)

Estimated Time: - Write indifference: 1-2 min (straightforward utility comparison) - Solve for x: 2-3 min (expanding (x-a)² - (1-a-x)², factoring, isolating x) - Write demands: 1 min (x = q1 by definition)

Subtotal: 4-6 minutes

Complexity: STANDARD (requires careful algebra with squared terms)

Part (b): Best responses in prices

Tasks: 1. FOC for Firm 1 2. Simplify to BR₁(p₂) 3. FOC for Firm 2 4. Simplify to BR₂(p₁)

Estimated Time: - FOC for Firm 1: 2-3 min (differentiation of π₁ = (p₁ - c₁)q₁, substitute ∂q₁/∂p₁ = -1/(2td), solve) - FOC for Firm 2: 2-3 min (symmetric)

Subtotal: 4-6 minutes

Complexity: STANDARD (routine FOC derivation, algebra similar to linear Hotelling but with td term)

Part (c): Price equilibrium

Tasks: 1. Solve 2×2 system of BR functions 2. Compute equilibrium market shares 3. Show price gap p₂* - p₁* = (c₂-c₁)/3 4. Explain how distance d affects average markup

Estimated Time: - Solve system: 3-4 min (substitute BR₁ into BR₂, back-substitute) - Compute shares: 2 min (substitute prices into demand) - Show price gap: 1 min (subtract equilibrium prices) - Discuss distance effect: 2 min (identify td term, explain economic mechanism)

Subtotal: 8-9 minutes

Complexity: STANDARD to CHALLENGING (system solving is routine, interpretation requires understanding differentiation)

Part (d): Interior condition (OPTIONAL CHALLENGE)

Tasks: 1. Derive condition |c₂-c₁| < 3td from q₁, q₂ ∈ (0,1) 2. Economic interpretation

Estimated Time: - Derive condition: 3-4 min (set q₁* < 1 and q₂* > 0, manipulate inequality) - Economic interpretation: 2 min (explain “cost difference too large”)

Subtotal: 5-6 minutes

Complexity: CHALLENGE (requires recognizing equilibrium validity conditions)

Note: Currently labeled “optional challenge” in line 78, which is good. However, many students may attempt it anyway if time permits.

Part (e): Numerical check

Tasks: Compute (p₁, p₂, q₁, q₂) with a=1/4, t=2, c₁=0, c₂=1

Estimated Time: - Calculate d = 1/2: 30 sec - Calculate td = 1: 30 sec - Compute p₁* = 4/3: 1 min - Compute p₂* = 5/3, q₁* = 2/3, q₂* = 1/3: 1-2 min

Subtotal: 2-3 minutes

Complexity: ROUTINE (substitution and arithmetic)

Part (f): Discussion question

Tasks: Explain why quadratic costs make competition depend on distance, unlike linear costs

Estimated Time: - Read and understand question: 1 min - Formulate answer using expressions from (c): 2-3 min

Subtotal: 2-3 minutes

Complexity: STANDARD (conceptual, requires understanding demand elasticity ∂q₁/∂p₁ = 1/(2td))

Total Time Estimates

Scenario 1: All parts including (d)

Sum: 4-6 + 4-6 + 8-9 + 5-6 + 2-3 + 2-3 = 25-33 minutes +10% reading buffer: 28-36 minutes Status: CRITICAL (40-80% over 20-minute target)

Scenario 2: Skip part (d) [recommended]

Sum: 4-6 + 4-6 + 8-9 + 2-3 + 2-3 = 20-27 minutes +10% buffer: 22-30 minutes Status: MEDIUM (10-50% over target)

Scenario 3: Only (a)-(c) [tightest time]

Sum: 4-6 + 4-6 + 8-9 = 16-21 minutes +10% buffer: 18-23 minutes Status: GOOD (0-15% over target, within acceptable range)

Comparison to Target

Scenario Min (min) Max (min) % of 20-min target Assessment

Skip (d) — (a,b,c,e,f) 22 30 110-150% MEDIUM: Slightly over

Target benchmark: 15-22 minutes (75-110% of 20-minute target) is EXCELLENT

Timing Issues

Issue 1: Too many sub-questions (CRITICAL)

Problem: 6 sub-questions (a-f) is excessive for a 20-minute exercise.

Evidence: - Even skipping optional (d), estimated time is 22-30 min - With (d), exercise takes 28-36 min (up to 80% over target)

Impact: Students will rush final parts or not finish, reducing learning value

Recommended Fix (choose one):

Option A (Remove numerical/discussion): - Keep (a), (b), (c), (d-optional) - Remove (e) and (f) - New total: 18-23 min ✓ GOOD

Option B (Make more optional): - Required: (a), (b), (c) only - Optional: (d), (e), (f) labeled “if time permits” - Core total: 18-23 min ✓ GOOD

Option C (Simplify part c): - Combine “show price gap” and “discuss distance” into one question - Keep (a), (b), (c-simplified), (e) - Remove (d) and (f) - New total: ~18-22 min ✓ GOOD

Issue 2: Part (c) is dense (MEDIUM)

Problem: Part (c) has 4 distinct tasks: 1. Solve equilibrium 2. Compute shares 3. Show price gap formula 4. Discuss distance effect

Evidence: Estimated 8-9 minutes for one “part” is high

Impact: May feel overwhelming; students might not finish all tasks

Recommended Fix: Split into (c) and (c’) or simplify: - (c) Solve for (p₁, p₂) and (q₁, q₂) - (c’) Show that p₂* - p₁* = (c₂-c₁)/3 and explain how distance affects markups

This makes progression clearer and allows partial credit.

Issue 3: No warm-up (LOW)

Problem: Part (a) immediately requires nontrivial algebra (expanding quadratics, factoring)

Evidence: Part (a) is 4-6 minutes, which is appropriate for a main derivation but quick for a warm-up

Impact: Groups may struggle to get started, eating into time for later parts

Recommended Fix (optional): Add a sub-part (a.0) before current (a): - (a.0) “Write down the utility functions for a consumer at location x buying from Firm 1 and Firm 2.” [1 min] - Then proceed to current (a)

This gives students a moment to orient before tackling the algebra.

Pedagogical Timing Notes

Strengths

  1. Logical progression: (a) → (b) → (c) is classic IO textbook sequence (demands → FOCs → equilibrium)
  2. Scaffolding: Earlier parts set up later parts (demands from (a) are used in (b), BRs from (b) used in (c))
  3. Validation: Part (e) numerical check helps students verify algebraic work

Weaknesses

  1. No breathing room: 25-33 min for a “15-minute” exercise (line 21) leaves no buffer for group discussion or questions
  2. Back-loaded complexity: Parts (d), (e), (f) come after the hardest work, when students are mentally fatigued
  3. Optional not clearly scoped: Part (d) says “optional challenge” but (e) and (f) are not marked optional, even though time will be tight

Recommendations

Priority 1 (CRITICAL): Reduce scope

Choose one:

  1. Remove (e) and (f) → Total: 18-23 min ✓
  2. Make (d), (e), (f) all optional → Core: 18-23 min ✓
  3. Keep only (a), (b), (c), (e) → Total: ~20-25 min (closer to target)

Priority 2 (MEDIUM): Clarify instructions

  1. Update line 21 from “≈15 minutes” to “≈20 minutes” to match actual scope
  2. Label optional parts explicitly:
    • “### (d) [OPTIONAL] When does one firm…”
    • “### (e) [OPTIONAL] Quick numerical check”
    • “### (f) [OPTIONAL] Discussion”

Priority 3 (LOW): Split part (c)

Make it two sub-parts (c) and (c’) for clarity and pacing.
