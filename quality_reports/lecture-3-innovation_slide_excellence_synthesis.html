Critical Issues (10)

C1. Three :contentReference[oaicite:N] ChatGPT artifacts (Lines 725, 735, 760)

All four reviewers flagged these. These OpenAI-format citation placeholders render as literal garbage in both RevealJS and Beamer. They must be removed and replaced with proper @citekey references or footnotes. - Line 725: EU Horizontal Merger Guidelines — needs a bib entry + @key - Line 735: Article 102 Guidance Paper — needs a bib entry + @key - Line 760: ACCC Merger Guidelines — needs a bib entry + @key

C2. Subject-verb disagreement in callout (Lines 590–591)

“The ‘consumer surplus vs total surplus’ are philosophical and political arguments: it changes…” Subject shifts from plural to singular. Proposed fix: “The choice between consumer surplus and total surplus is not merely technical but philosophical and political: it determines (i) which efficiencies matter, (ii) how evidence is organized, and (iii) which cases are brought.”

C3. Three hardcoded author-year citations without @key (Lines 585–587)

  • Line 585: “Neven & Röller” → should be @nevenConsumerSurplusVs2005
  • Line 586: “Farrell & Katz” → should be @farrellEconomicsWelfareStandards
  • Line 587: “Williamson” → should be @williamsonEconomiesAntitrustDefense1972

C4. Williamson date discrepancy: slide says 1968, bib entry is 1972

The bib entry williamsonEconomiesAntitrustDefense1972 cites the 1972 Rowley collection reprint. The original AER paper is 1968. Either update the bib entry to the 1968 original or add a new entry. Pandoc will render “1972” which conflicts with the hardcoded “1968” in the slide text.

Medium Issues (12)

M1. farrellEconomicsWelfareStandards bib entry missing year field

Pandoc will render as “Farrell and Katz (n.d.)”. The paper was published in Competition Policy International, 2006, Vol. 2, No. 2. Add year = 2006, volume = {2}, number = {2}, pages = {3--28}.

M2. “Consumer welfare” conflated with “consumer surplus” (Line 596)

The slide equates “consumer welfare / consumer surplus (CS)” without noting that in US antitrust, Bork’s “consumer welfare” actually meant total welfare. At MSc level, this distinction matters. Add a brief clarification.

M3. Section numbering inconsistency (Line 692)

“# 4. Williamson…” but no preceding sections numbered 1–3. Either number all four anchors or remove “4.”

M4. Citekey-in-title overflow risk (Lines 625, 658)

Raw @nevenConsumerSurplusVs2005 and @farrellEconomicsWelfareStandards in ## headings will expand to long rendered citations, risking title overflow. Use short descriptive titles and cite in body text.

M5. Missing diagram despite title reference (Line 694)

“Williamson (1968): the trade-off diagram, formalized” — but no diagram is present. Either add the Williamson partial-equilibrium diagram or rename the slide to remove “diagram.”

M6. Common-agency title truncated (Line 625)

“…as a common-agency” — missing noun. Should be “common-agency problem” or “common-agency model.”

M7. Jurisdiction slides repetitive structure (Lines 718–764)

EU (×2), Canada, Australia — four consecutive slides with near-identical bullet-list structure. Consider consolidating into a comparison table + an “operational implications” slide.

M8. “Common agency” model never briefly defined (Line 625)

Students encounter the term without even a one-sentence sketch of the Bernheim-Whinston common-agency framework.

M9. Bare “Kaplow” without (key?) on line 605

“The canonical welfare-econ logic in Kaplow:” — should use @ChoiceWelfareStandards.

M10. Bare “Kaplow’s” in slide title (Line 594)

“Kaplow’s choosing a welfare standard” — consider “Choosing a welfare standard (Kaplow 2011)” or similar.

M11. Canada slide missing citation (Line 747)

The Bureau’s Merger Enforcement Guidelines (2011) are mentioned but not cited. Other jurisdiction slides attempt citations (albeit broken ones).

M12. Informal abbreviation “welfare-econ” (Line 605)

Should be “welfare-economics.”
