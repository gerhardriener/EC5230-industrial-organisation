ILO Mapping — EC5230 Final Exam (Main Paper, 2025-26)

Module: EC5230 Industrial Organisation Paper: Final Exam — Main Diet (May 2026) Duration: 2 hours Total marks: 100

Intended Learning Outcomes

ILO Description
ILO 1 Understand how IO deepens the understanding of firm/agent behaviour in imperfectly competitive markets.
ILO 2 Select the right model(s) to analyse various issues in industrial economics.
ILO 3 Analyse economic behaviour through game theory.
ILO 4 Develop curiosity by identifying interesting papers and examples to extend knowledge of the subject.

Note: ILO 4 is primarily assessed through the policy report coursework. It is included below only where a question genuinely invites students to draw on wider reading or policy implications.

Sub-Part-Level ILO Mapping

Question 1: Cournot oligopoly and mergers (20 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
(a) Derive symmetric \(n\)-firm Cournot equilibrium 5 ILO 2, ILO 3 Derivation
(b) Numerical example \(n=3\): CS, PS, DWL 5 ILO 1, ILO 2 Calculation, welfare analysis
(c) Post-merger equilibrium (2+1 firm) 5 ILO 2, ILO 3 Derivation, calculation
(d) Merger profitability; merger paradox 5 ILO 1, ILO 2 Calculation, conceptual discussion

Question 2: Salop circular city (20 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
(a) Marginal consumer and demand 4 ILO 2, ILO 3 Derivation
(b) Symmetric Nash equilibrium price 4 ILO 2, ILO 3 Derivation
(c) Free-entry equilibrium \(n^*\) 6 ILO 1, ILO 2 Derivation, interpretation
(d) Social optimum \(n^{**}\) and over-entry 6 ILO 1, ILO 2 Derivation, welfare reasoning

Question 3: Innovation incentives and patent policy (20 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
(a) Drastic/non-drastic classification 4 ILO 2 Calculation, condition evaluation
(b) WTP ranking: monopoly, competitive, social 8 ILO 1, ILO 2 Calculation, ordering
(c) Arrow’s replacement effect 4 ILO 1 Economic interpretation
(d) Optimal patent length (Scotchmer model) 4 ILO 1, ILO 4 Calculation, policy discussion

Question 4A: Strategic delegation (10 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
(a) Stage-2 equilibrium quantities given \(\theta\) 3 ILO 2, ILO 3 Derivation
(b) Optimal delegation parameter \(\hat\theta\) and equilibrium 4 ILO 2, ILO 3 Derivation, backward induction
(c) Profit comparison; prisoner’s dilemma 3 ILO 1, ILO 3 Calculation, game-theoretic interpretation

Question 4B: Research joint ventures (10 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
(d) Non-cooperative expected payoff \(V_N\) 3 ILO 2, ILO 3 Expected-value calculation
(e) RJV expected payoff \(V_C\) 3 ILO 2, ILO 3 Expected-value calculation
(f) Range of \(F\) where RJV preferred; policy 4 ILO 1, ILO 4 Algebraic comparison, policy discussion

Question 5A: Bundling (12 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
(a) Separate selling: optimal prices and profit 4 ILO 2 Calculation
(b) Pure bundling: optimal price and profit 4 ILO 2 Calculation
(c) Comparison: profit, welfare, intuition 4 ILO 1, ILO 2 Welfare analysis, interpretation

Question 5B: Advertising — Dorfman-Steiner (8 marks)

Part Description Marks ILOs Method
