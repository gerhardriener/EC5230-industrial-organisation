Advertising: views / types

Consider three views on advertising:

  • Persuasive
    • changes consumers’ tastes
    • amplifies product differentiation and brand loyalty
  • Informative
    • informs products’ existence, price, characteristics
    • advertising expenditure signals high quality
  • Complementary
    • changes consumers’ utility function
    • complements products and conveys certain images

Advertising under monopoly

Advertising under monopoly: Dorfman-Steiner model

  • Monopoly chooses price-advertising decision \((p, A) \ge 0\).
  • Demand is \(Q(p,A)\) with \(\partial Q/\partial p < 0\) and \(\partial Q/\partial A > 0\).
  • Cost: \(C(Q(p,A))\) with marginal cost \(C' > 0\).

\[ \max_{p,A} \Pi(p,A) = pQ(p,A) - C(Q(p,A)) - A \]

\[ \frac{p - C'}{p} = \frac{1}{\eta_{Q,p}} \]

\[ \frac{A}{pQ} = \frac{\eta_{Q,A}}{\eta_{Q,p}} \]

Advertising under monopoly: persuasive

\[ Q(p,A) = 1 - \frac{p}{g(A)} \]

with \(g(A)=1+\sqrt{A}\).

Advertising under monopoly: welfare analysis example

\[ A^M = \left(\frac{1}{8}\right)^2, \quad p^M = \frac{9}{16}, \quad Q^M = \frac{1}{2} \]

\[ A^* = \left(\frac{3}{16}\right)^2 \]
